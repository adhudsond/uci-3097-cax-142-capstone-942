Password Reset & Account Lockout Handling

1. A user emails the IT helpdesk inbox saying they're locked out of their account.
2. The helpdesk inbox is checked manually by whichever technician is free, roughly every 30-60 minutes.
3. The technician replies asking the user to confirm their identity by stating their employee ID and manager's name.
4. The user replies (often hours later), and the technician re-checks the inbox to find the reply.
5. The technician manually looks up the employee in Active Directory to verify the details match.
6. The technician resets the password to a temporary one and unlocks the account.
7. The technician emails the temporary password to the user in plain text.
8. The technician manually logs the incident in a separate spreadsheet for the monthly report.
9. The user must contact IT again if the temporary password expires before they log in.

Pain points: no self-service option, identity verification is informal and insecure,
temporary passwords are sent in plain text over email, response time depends on when
someone happens to check the shared inbox, and logging is manual and disconnected
from the ticketing system.
